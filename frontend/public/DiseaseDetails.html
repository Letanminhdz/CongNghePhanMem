Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

A chronic condition that affects the way your body metabolizes sugar (glucose) — an important source of fuel for your body. With type 2 diabetes, your body either resists the effects of insulin or doesn't produce enough insulin to maintain normal glucose levels.

Immediate Warning Signs

Seek emergency medical care if you experience any of the following symptoms, which could indicate diabetic ketoacidosis or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state:

  • Fruity-scented breath
  • Confusion or delirium
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Shortness of breath

Common Symptoms

Increased Thirst & Urination

Excess sugar building up in your bloodstream causes fluid to be pulled from tissues.

Increased Hunger & Fatigue

Without enough insulin to move sugar into cells, your muscles and organs become energy-depleted.

Blurred Vision

If blood sugar is too high, fluid may be pulled from the lenses of your eyes.

Slow-healing Sores

High blood sugar can affect blood flow and cause nerve damage, making healing harder.

Causes

  • Insulin Resistance: Muscle, liver and fat cells don't use insulin properly.
  • Genetics: Family history increases risk significantly.
  • Weight: Being overweight is a primary risk factor.
  • Inactivity: Less physical activity means higher risk.

Prevention

  • Healthy Diet: Focus on lower fat, calories and higher fiber.
  • Active Lifestyle: Aim for 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly.
  • Weight Loss: Losing 7-10% of body weight can reduce risk.
  • Avoid Inactivity: Try not to sit still for long periods.
